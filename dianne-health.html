🦴 Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
✅ Pretty much in remission!

Your rheumatologist says you are doing very well and are almost completely in remission. This means the disease activity is very low. Keep taking your medications as prescribed so this continues.

🫀 Carotid Artery Narrowing
⚠️ Being monitored by your doctors

The carotid arteries are the main vessels that carry blood to your brain. One side is significantly narrowed, while the other has only minor narrowing. Your doctors are aware and watching this.

Call 911 immediately if you suddenly experience:
  • Weakness or numbness on one side of your face, arm, or leg
  • Trouble speaking or understanding words
  • Vision problems in one or both eyes
  • A very sudden, severe headache
  • Dizziness or loss of balance suddenly

These could be signs of a stroke — every second counts.

🩺 High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)
💊 Managed with medication

High blood pressure means the heart has to work harder than it should. Your new Irbesartan prescription helps keep your blood pressure at a safe level. Taking it every day without missing is very important — especially with the carotid artery narrowing.
